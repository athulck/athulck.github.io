Scanning & Enumeration

Once you know a target exists, you find its open doors. Scanning and enumeration turn a map into a list of concrete, reachable services — the things you can actually try.

What is scanning?

Scanning is active reconnaissance: you send packets to a target and listen to what comes back. Where recon told you what exists, scanning tells you what's reachable and responding.

Mindset: Take it one phase at a time — each builds on the last.

Ports & services

Every networked service listens on a port. A port scan asks each port "are you open?" and notes the replies. Open ports are doors; the service behind each one is what you'll study next.

Enumeration

Enumeration goes a layer deeper: not just "port 80 is open" but "it's running a specific web server, with these paths, and this banner." The richer your enumeration, the clearer your attack surface.

# Classic first scan against a lab target you own
nmap -sV -p- 10.10.10.5      # all ports, detect versions
nmap --top-ports 100 10.10.10.5
# Then enumerate what you found
Stay legal: Scanning is active — it touches the target and is logged. Only scan systems you own or have written permission to test.

Wrap-up

You now understand the jump from recon to scanning, what ports and services are, and why enumeration is where real detail lives. That's the surface of phase two.
