Reporting & Methodology

The skill that actually pays. A brilliant exploit means nothing if you can't explain it. Reporting and methodology turn what you found into something a client can understand and fix.

Why reporting matters

Clients don't pay for access — they pay for clarity: what's wrong, how bad it is, and how to fix it. Your report is the product. It's also exactly what certifications like OSCP grade you on.

Mindset: Take it one phase at a time — each builds on the last.

Having a methodology

A methodology is a repeatable process so you don't miss things under pressure: recon to scan to exploit to escalate to document. Following a consistent flow is what separates professionals from lucky guessers.

Notes & evidence

Great pentesters document as they go — commands, screenshots, timestamps. At L0, build the habit early: if it isn't written down with evidence, it didn't happen.

Wrap-up

You now understand why the report is the real deliverable, why a repeatable methodology matters, and why disciplined note-taking underpins everything. That's the surface of professional practice.
