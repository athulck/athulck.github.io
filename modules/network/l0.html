Network Exploitation

A foothold on one machine is rarely the goal. Network exploitation is about understanding how systems talk to each other — and how a single weak link lets you move through the whole environment.

Thinking in networks

Real targets are rarely a single box. They're networks: servers, workstations, and services that trust each other. Understanding those trust relationships is the core skill here.

Mindset: Take it one phase at a time — each builds on the last.

Protocols & misconfigurations

Protocols like SMB, RDP, and SSH are everywhere — and often misconfigured. Default credentials, exposed shares, and weak settings are the bread and butter of network attacks.

Pivoting & lateral movement

Once inside, attackers pivot: using one compromised host to reach others that weren't exposed to the internet. At L0, just hold the mental model — access spreads through trust.

# Enumerate common network services (lab environment)
nmap -p 22,139,445,3389 10.10.10.0/24
# Open SMB? RDP? Each is a relationship worth understanding
Stay legal: Network attacks are noisy and high-impact. Confine all practice to isolated lab networks you control.

Wrap-up

You now see targets as networks, understand why protocol misconfigurations matter, and grasp the idea of lateral movement. That's the surface of network exploitation.
