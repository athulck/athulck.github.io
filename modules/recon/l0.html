Reconnaissance & Footprinting

Before an attacker touches a target, they study it. Reconnaissance is the quiet, patient first phase of every engagement — and getting it right is what separates noise from a real attack path.

What is reconnaissance?

Reconnaissance (or recon) is the process of gathering information about a target system, network, or organisation. The goal is to build a map: what exists, what's exposed, and where the soft edges might be — all before launching any attack.

Mindset: A pentester spends more time looking than touching. Good recon makes every later step faster and quieter.

Passive vs active recon

There are two broad styles, and knowing the difference keeps you both effective and legal:

  • Passive recon — you gather information without directly interacting with the target's systems. Think public records, search engines, and leaked data. The target never sees you.
  • Active recon — you interact directly: pinging hosts, resolving services, probing. It's richer, but it leaves traces.

OSINT basics

OSINT — Open-Source Intelligence — is the heart of passive recon. You'd be surprised how much an organisation publishes about itself without realising it: employee names, technologies in job posts, subdomains in certificate logs, and more.

A simple starting toolkit:

# A few passive starting points (read-only, public data)
whois example.com
host example.com
# Certificate transparency often reveals subdomains
# Search engines with operators: site:example.com filetype:pdf
Stay legal: Only ever run recon against systems you own or have explicit written permission to test. L0 is about understanding — practise on lab targets, never on systems you don't control.

DNS & WHOIS

DNS is a goldmine. It translates names to addresses, and along the way it leaks structure: mail servers, subdomains, and sometimes internal naming conventions. WHOIS records can reveal registration details and infrastructure ownership.

At L0 you only need the intuition: DNS = the org's address book, and reading it carefully tells you where things live.

Wrap-up

You now know what recon is, the difference between passive and active approaches, and why OSINT and DNS matter. That's the surface — enough to understand every phase that follows.
