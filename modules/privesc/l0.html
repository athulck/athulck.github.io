Privilege Escalation

Getting in is half the story. Privilege escalation is the art of turning a limited foothold into full control — from a low-privilege user to root or SYSTEM.

From foothold to control

Your first access is usually weak: a low-privilege account that can't do much. Privilege escalation is the process of climbing from there to administrative control.

Mindset: Take it one phase at a time — each builds on the last.

Common vectors

Misconfigured permissions, outdated software, stored credentials, and overly-trusting scheduled tasks are classic paths upward. The skill is methodical enumeration of what the current user can touch.

Linux vs Windows

Both operating systems escalate differently — Linux leans on SUID binaries and misconfigs; Windows on services and tokens. At L0, the takeaway is that the method is the same: enumerate, then exploit a weakness.

# Linux: quick look at what the current user can do (lab box)
id
sudo -l            # what can we run as root?
find / -perm -4000 2>/dev/null   # SUID binaries
Stay legal: Privilege escalation gives you full control of a machine. Only ever do this on lab systems you own or are authorised to test.

Wrap-up

You now understand why initial access is rarely enough, the common categories of escalation, and that careful enumeration drives it all. That's the surface of privesc.
